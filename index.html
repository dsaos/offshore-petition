Keep our seas shining

"Reversing offshore safety rules isn’t just deregulation, it’s willful ignorance."
– Center for Biological Diversity

On April 20, 2010, BP's Deepwater Horizon offshore drilling rig suffered from a blowout—oil spewed three stories high from its base, and quickly ignited. Due to lax safety checks, lack of emergency protocol, and aging equipment, the rig was entirely destroyed within 2 days.

11 workers were killed. 5 million barrels' worth of crude oil decimated the entire Gulf of Mexico coastline and ecosystem. Oil sheens can still be seen today, and 90% of newborn acquatic life in the area demonstrate abnormalities.

In the years following, the US government established the BSEE, charged with enacting regulations to routinely inspect key failsafe equipment and maintain thorough emergency procedure.

Now, the Trump administration's Department of Interior has charged the BSEE with repealing the same regulations they made to prevent these catastrophes. They have already eliminated requirements for third-party reviews of key safety mechanisms, and are planning more.
